Spectacled Bears

      The Spectacled Bear is a short faced bear, known for their golden/yellow markings around their face. They are most commonly found in northern and western South America. Here on this page, you will learn more information on this unique bear's background, and see live photos of the bears along with fun facts!

Where Does Their Name Come From?

Spectacled Bears are given their name because of the rings of yellow or gold circling around their eyes, like spectacles. Another name you may refer to these bears as is an Andean Bear, because of their habitation in the Andes Mountain!


Where Do They Live?

These bears enjoy mostly rain forests, grasslands, and scrublands(lands consisting of a scrub vegetation.) They prefer mountainous areas due to the range of altitudes. You can find them climbing trees and forming large nests/platforms on branches to eat and rest there.

Countries where you could find Spectacled Bears:

  • Peru
  • Venezuela
  • Ecuador
  • Colombia
  • Bolivia

How Big are They?

The Spectacled Bear's length ranges from 4 to 6 feet long! An estimated weight for males is around 340, while females make up only half of that weight! The cubs are born with a weight of about 300 grams. They grow very quickly though, leaving their mother a year after their birth.

Save These Bears!

Sadly, these adorable bears' conservation is uncertain. The rate of population for them decreases 2-4 percent a year. Thankfully, organizations are being arranged, and are working with zoos and conservatories to preserve the Spectacled Bears existence. Donate here!
